Hi! I'm currently a Ph.D. student at Yonsei University, advised by Prof. Hyung-Chan An. I received my B.S. degree in Computer Science from Yonsei University.

My major research interests reside in theoretical computer science. I'm particularly interested in (but not limited to) online algorithms (e.g., online matching) and approximation algorithms (e.g., facility location and traveling salesman). I recently got to know algorithms with predictions and am studying this field, too.

I'm also eager to convey theoretical aspects of computer science to others, specifically in Korean. To this end, I'm running a blog where I post several related topics including basics to recent work. Visit my blog 「Gazelle and Computer Science」 (Korean) if interested.

I'm currently visiting Cornell University, hosted by Prof. David Shmoys. It is my great pleasure and honor to have this opportunity to visit here and communicate with other brilliant researchers here. I'll be staying here until mid December.
